About Me

I am a PhD Candidate at the Institute of Service Science, National Tsing Hua University, specializing in AI-enabled knowledge management systems and human-AI collaboration. My research explores how generative AI and multi-agent systems can transform knowledge ecosystems and enhance organizational learning through intelligent service design.

My interdisciplinary approach bridges advanced AI technologies with service science, combining action design research with cutting-edge computational techniques including process mining, natural language processing, and agentic AI development. I have published at leading conferences such as HICSS and KMO, with research featured in international journals.

Current AI Research Focus

Pioneering the development of cooperative multi-agent knowledge service systems that seamlessly integrate human expertise with AI capabilities. My work explores how generative AI can evolve from simple assistants to intelligent allies in complex knowledge ecosystems, enabling more effective human-AI collaboration and organizational learning.
